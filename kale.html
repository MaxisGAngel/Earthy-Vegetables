How to grow Kale

A Step by step Guide

Kale is a hardy leafy green that thrives in cool temperatures and is packed with nutrients. It is easy to grow and can be harvested multiple times throughout the season. Follow this guide to successfully cultivate your own fresh kale.

Difficulty Rating: Easy to Moderate
Best Season to Grow: Spring and Fall
Can Be Grown: Both Indoors and Outdoors

Supplies Needed to Grow Kale

  • Kale seeds or seedlings

  • Well-draining, fertile soil

  • Compost or balanced fertilizer (10-10-10)

  • Garden trowel

  • Watering can or hose

  • Mulch (straw, leaves, or wood chips)

  • Raised garden bed or containers (for small spaces)

Setting Up Your Kale Garden

Choosing the Right Location

  • Sunlight: Kale thrives in full sun to partial shade, needing at least 4-6 hours of sunlight per day.

  • Soil: Use rich, well-draining soil with a pH between 6.0 and 7.5.

  • Spacing: Plant kale 12-18 inches apart in rows spaced 18-24 inches apart for proper air circulation.

  • Indoor Setup: If growing indoors, use deep containers (at least 12 inches deep) with nutrient-rich soil.


Preparing the Soil

  • Loosen the soil to a depth of at least 12 inches for strong root development.

  • Mix compost into the soil to enhance fertility and improve moisture retention.

  • Avoid planting kale in the same spot as other brassica family plants (such as cabbage or broccoli) to prevent disease buildup.


Planting Kale

  • Direct Sowing or Transplanting: Start seeds indoors 4-6 weeks before the last frost or sow seeds directly into the garden 2-4 weeks before the last frost.

  • Water Immediately: After planting, water thoroughly to help seedlings establish.


Caring for Your Kale Plants

Watering

  • Keep the soil consistently moist, providing 1-1.5 inches of water per week.

  • Water at the base of the plant to prevent fungal diseases.

Fertilizing

  • Apply a balanced fertilizer every 4-6 weeks to support steady growth.

  • Avoid excessive nitrogen, which can cause overly tender leaves that attract pests.

Mulching & Weed Control

  • Apply mulch around kale plants to retain moisture and suppress weeds.

  • Hand-pull weeds carefully to avoid disturbing kale roots.

Pest and Disease Prevention

  • Watch for pests such as aphids, cabbage worms, and flea beetles.

  • Use floating row covers to protect young plants from pests.

  • Rotate crops yearly to prevent diseases like powdery mildew and clubroot.

Harvesting

  • Kale is ready to harvest in 50-75 days, depending on the variety.

  • Pick the outer leaves first, allowing the inner leaves to continue growing.

  • Regular harvesting encourages continuous leaf production.
