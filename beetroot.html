How to grow Beetroot

A Step by step Guide

Beetroot is a versatile and nutritious vegetable that is easy to grow and provides both edible roots and leafy greens. Whether grown in a garden bed or a container, beetroot is a rewarding plant with a quick harvest time. Follow this guide to successfully grow healthy and flavorful beetroot at home.

Difficulty Rating: Easy to Moderate
Best Season to Grow: Spring and Fall
Can Be Grown: Both Indoors and Outdoors

Supplies Needed to Grow Beetroot

  • Beetroot seeds

  • Well-draining, nutrient-rich soil

  • Compost or balanced fertilizer (10-10-10)

  • Garden trowel

  • Watering can or hose

  • Mulch (straw or wood chips)

  • Raised garden bed or deep containers (for small spaces)

Setting Up Your Beetroot Garden

Choosing the Right Location

  • Sunlight: Beetroot thrives in full sun to partial shade, requiring at least 4-6 hours of sunlight per day.

  • Soil: Use loose, well-draining soil rich in organic matter, with a pH between 6.0 and 7.5.

  • Spacing: Sow seeds 1 inch apart in rows spaced 12 inches apart. Thin seedlings to 3-4 inches apart once they sprout.

  • Indoor Setup: If growing indoors, use deep containers (at least 8 inches deep) to allow for proper root growth.


Preparing the Soil

  • Loosen the soil to a depth of at least 12 inches to encourage root development.

  • Mix compost into the soil to provide essential nutrients.

  • Avoid stony or compacted soil, as it can lead to misshapen roots.


Planting Beetroot

  • Direct Sowing: Plant seeds Â½ inch deep and 1 inch apart in well-prepared soil.

  • Succession Planting: Sow seeds every 2-3 weeks for a continuous harvest.

  • Thinning: Once seedlings are 2-3 inches tall, thin them to 3-4 inches apart to allow room for root growth.


Caring for Your Beetroot Plants

Watering

  • Keep the soil evenly moist but not waterlogged, providing 1 inch of water per week.

  • Water at the base of the plant to prevent fungal diseases.

Fertilizing

  • Apply a balanced fertilizer once at planting time.

  • Avoid high nitrogen fertilizers, as they encourage leaf growth instead of root development.

Mulching & Weed Control

  • Apply mulch around plants to retain moisture and suppress weeds.

  • Regularly remove weeds to prevent competition for nutrients.

Pest and Disease Prevention

  • Watch for pests such as leaf miners, aphids, and flea beetles.

  • Use insecticidal soap or neem oil to deter pests.

  • Rotate crops yearly to prevent soil-borne diseases like root rot and mildew.

Harvesting

  • Beetroot is ready to harvest in 50-70 days, depending on the variety.

  • Pull up roots when they reach 1.5-3 inches in diameter for the best flavor.

  • Harvest beet greens when they are 4-6 inches long for salads or cooking.
