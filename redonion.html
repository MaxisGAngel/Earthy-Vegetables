How to grow Red Onion

A Step by step Guide

Red onions are a vibrant and flavorful ingredient in many dishes. They are easy to grow in a variety of climates with the right setup and care. Follow this guide to learn how to grow healthy and delicious red onions at home.

Difficulty Rating: Easy to Moderate
Best Season to Grow: Spring and Fall
Can Be Grown: Both Indoors and Outdoors

Supplies Needed to Grow Red Onion

  • Red onion seeds, sets, or seedlings

  • Well-draining, nutrient-rich soil

  • Compost or balanced fertilizer (10-10-10)

  • Garden trowel

  • Watering can or hose

  • Mulch (straw or wood chips)

  • Raised garden bed or deep containers (for small spaces)

Setting Up Your Red Onion Garden

Choosing the Right Location

  • Sunlight: Red onions require 6-8 hours of full sun per day.

  • Soil: Ensure the soil is well-draining and rich in organic matter with a pH between 6.0 and 7.0.

  • Spacing: Plant onions 4-6 inches apart, with rows spaced 12-18 inches apart.

  • Indoor Setup: If growing indoors, use deep containers (at least 6 inches deep) and place them in a sunny window or under grow lights.


Preparing the Soil

  • Loosen the soil to a depth of at least 8-12 inches to encourage root growth.

  • Mix compost into the soil to provide essential nutrients.

  • Avoid heavy, compacted soils that can hinder bulb formation.


Planting Red Onion

  • From Seeds: Sow seeds Â¼ inch deep indoors 8-10 weeks before the last frost and transplant outdoors when seedlings are sturdy.

  • From Sets: Plant red onion sets 1 inch deep with the pointed end facing upward.

  • From Seedlings: Transplant seedlings when they are 4-6 inches tall.


Caring for Your Red Onion Plants

Watering

  • Keep the soil evenly moist but not waterlogged, providing 1 inch of water per week.

  • Reduce watering as onions mature to encourage bulb development.

Fertilizing

  • Apply a nitrogen-rich fertilizer every 2-3 weeks during early growth stages.

  • Stop fertilizing once bulbs begin to form.

Mulching & Weed Control

  • Apply mulch around plants to retain moisture and suppress weeds.

  • Regularly remove weeds to prevent competition for nutrients.

Pest and Disease Prevention

  • Watch for pests such as onion thrips and maggots.

  • Use insecticidal soap or neem oil to deter pests.

  • Rotate crops yearly to prevent soil-borne diseases like onion rot.

Harvesting

  • Red onions are ready to harvest in 90-120 days, depending on the variety.

  • Harvest when the tops begin to yellow and fall over.

  • Gently pull onions from the soil and let them cure in a dry, well-ventilated area for 2-3 weeks before storing.
