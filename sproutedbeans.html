How to grow Sprouted Beans

A Step by step Guide

Sprouted beans are nutritious, easy to grow, and require minimal effort. They can be grown indoors at any time of the year, making them a perfect addition to a healthy diet. Follow this guide to successfully grow your own sprouted beans.

Difficulty Rating: Easy
Best Season to Grow: Year-round
Can Be Grown: Indoors

Supplies Needed to Grow Sprouted Beans

  • Dried beans (mung beans, lentils, chickpeas, etc.)

  • A glass jar or sprouting tray

  • A mesh lid or cheesecloth with a rubber band

  • Clean, filtered water

  • A strainer

Setting Up Your Sprouted Beans Garden

Choosing the Right Beans

  • Use organic, untreated dried beans for the best results.

  • Mung beans, lentils, chickpeas, and alfalfa seeds are excellent choices for sprouting.


Preparing the beans

  • Rinse the beans thoroughly with clean water to remove any debris.

  • Soak the beans in a large jar filled with filtered water for 8-12 hours (or overnight).

  • Drain the beans using a mesh lid or strainer.

Caring for Your Sprouted Beans Plants

Sprouting Process

  • Place the drained beans back in the jar and cover with a mesh lid or cheesecloth.

  • Keep the jar in a cool, dark place (such as a cupboard) for the first 1-2 days.

  • Rinse and drain the beans twice a day (morning and evening) to prevent mold growth.

Light and Airflow

  • Once the beans start sprouting (after 2-3 days), move the jar to a bright, indirect light area to encourage chlorophyll development.

  • Ensure good airflow to prevent spoilage.

Harvesting

  • Most sprouted beans are ready to eat within 3-5 days.

  • Once they have reached your desired length, give them a final rinse and store them in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.
